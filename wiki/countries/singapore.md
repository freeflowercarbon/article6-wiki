# Singapore 🇸🇬 | シンガポール — Article 6.2 Acquiring Country

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    Singapore is one of the **most advanced Article 6.2 acquiring countries**, using international carbon credits (ICCs) to supplement its domestic carbon tax. The carbon tax (S$25/t in 2024–25, rising to S$45/t in 2026–27) creates structural demand for ICCs, capped at **5% of each liable entity's obligations**. Singapore's Implementation Agreement (IA) framework is widely cited as the **international standard for Article 6.2 design**.

    | Item | Detail |
    |------|--------|
    | NDC Target | 60% reduction below 2005 by 2030; net zero 2050 |
    | Carbon Tax (2024–25) | S$25/t CO₂e (≈ US$19/t) |
    | Carbon Tax (2026–27) | S$45/t CO₂e (≈ US$33/t) |
    | ICC Procurement Limit | Max 5% of each entity's carbon tax obligation |
    | Primary Mechanism | Implementation Agreement (IA) + private-sector RFP |
    | Key Ministry | NEA (National Environment Agency), MSE |

    ## Reversal Risk Policy (Singapore IA)

    | Element | Singapore IA Approach |
    |---------|----------------------|
    | Buffer pool requirement | Required; project-specific risk assessment by NEA |
    | Published criteria | NEA Eligibility Criteria include permanence as explicit standard |
    | Post-buffer obligation | Host country government must provide replacement ITMOs |
    | Singapore's NDC treatment | Singapore retains registered ITMOs; host country replaces losses |
    | Risk management verification | NEA requires multi-year monitoring and independent verification |

    > Source: NEA ICC Eligibility Criteria: <https://www.nea.gov.sg/our-services/climate-change-energy-efficiency/singapore-s-international-climate-change-cooperation/international-cooperation-on-climate-change>

    ## Methodology Approach (Singapore IA)

    Singapore uses a **country-specific Eligibility List** system: for each IA, Singapore and the host country bilaterally agree which carbon crediting programmes and methodologies are eligible.[^sgmeth]

    | Dimension | Singapore IA Approach |
    |-----------|----------------------|
    | Registry type | Approved registries (positive list), not proprietary methodologies |
    | Approved registries | Verra VCS, Gold Standard, American Carbon Registry (ACR), ART TREES, Global Carbon Council [^sgmeth2] |
    | Methodology selection | Bilateral — each IA has its own approved methodology list |
    | CCP requirement | Not mandatory, but highly valued; aligned criteria |
    | Key constraint | CA (Corresponding Adjustment) mandatory for all approved projects |
    | Special protocol | Verra + Gold Standard published joint Article 6.2 crediting protocol for Singapore [^sgproto] |

    [^sgmeth]: Singapore NEA Eligibility Criteria: <https://www.nea.gov.sg/media/news/news/index/singapore-publishes-eligibility-list-for-international-carbon-credits-under-the-carbon-tax-regime>; MSE: <https://www.mse.gov.sg/latest-news/eligibility-criteria-for-internationalcarboncredits/>
    [^sgmeth2]: Carbon Pulse, "Singapore adds ACR, ART TREES standards": <https://carbon-pulse.com/195068/>
    [^sgproto]: Verra + Gold Standard joint protocol for Singapore: <https://verra.org/singapore-gold-standard-and-verra-publish-article-6-2-crediting-protocol/>

    ## CCP (ICVCM Core Carbon Principles) Alignment

    Singapore's NEA Eligibility Criteria closely parallel ICVCM's CCP in many respects:
    - Requires robust additionality and baseline assessment
    - Requires Corresponding Adjustment (CA) by host government
    - Requires third-party verification
    - Permanence and reversal risk management explicit

    CCP certification is not required by NEA, but projects meeting CCP standards are well-positioned for Singapore IA procurement.

    > ICVCM: <https://icvcm.org/the-core-carbon-principles/>

    ## サマリー
    シンガポールはArticle 6.2において**最も積極的な調達国の一つ**。国内カーボン税（2024〜2025年：S$25/t、2026〜2027年：S$45/t）の補完手段として国際炭素クレジット（ICC）を活用。IA設計はArticle 6.2の国際標準として注目。

    ## 基本情報
    | 項目 | 内容 |
    |------|------|
    | NDC目標 | 2030年に2005年比60%削減（ネットゼロ2050） |
    | 炭素税率（2024〜2025） | S$25/t-CO2e |
    | 炭素税率（2026〜2027） | S$45/t-CO2e |
    | ICC上限 | 炭素税義務の最大5%をICCで代替可能 |
    | 主管省庁 | MSE（環境持続可能性省）、NEA |
    | Article 6.2の役割 | 調達国（ITMO取得側） |

    ## NDC達成見込み・グローバルストックテイク
