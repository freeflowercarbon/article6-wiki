# Uganda 🇺🇬 | ウガンダ — Article 6.2 Host Country

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    Uganda is **East Africa's most carbon-credit-rich country**, with a voluntary carbon market portfolio of 33M+ credits and REDD+ as the backbone of its NDC (82.7% of mitigation from AFOLU). The **National Climate Change (Climate Change Mechanisms) Regulations 2025** (enacted April 2025) established a formal legal framework for Article 6.2 authorization. Switzerland signed a bilateral agreement at **COP30 (November 2025)**. However, a structural tension exists: Uganda's primary carbon asset is **NBS/REDD+**, while Switzerland explicitly **excludes forestry and biological sequestration** from its ITMO eligibility. This means the Switzerland bilateral is likely to focus on clean cooking, renewable energy, or efficiency projects — not Uganda's largest carbon stock.

    ## NDC Achievement Status

    > Uganda's NDC is **heavily AFOLU-dependent** — 82.7% of the 36.75 MtCO₂e target comes from land use, forestry, and agriculture. This creates both the country's core carbon asset (REDD+, reforestation) and its structural risk: AFOLU-sector ITMOs are precisely what Switzerland excludes. Forest restoration target: 2.5M ha by 2030.[^ugndcstatus]

    ## Article 6.2 Regulatory Framework (2025 Regulations)

    The **National Climate Change (Climate Change Mechanisms) Regulations 2025** (enacted April 2025) established Uganda's formal carbon market legal framework:[^ugregs]

    - **Project approval**: Formal request to Minister → Letter of No Objection → authorization
    - **Verification**: Only accredited verifiers under internationally recognized standards (Verra VCS, Gold Standard) permitted
    - **International transfers**: Require additional authorization; double-counting prevention provisions
    - **Benefit-sharing**: Mandatory benefit-sharing plans for host communities
    - **Oversight body**: Ministry of Water and Environment (MWE) + multi-sectoral technical committee

    > ⚠️ Institutional gap noted (as of 2025): "We have players operating who are selling carbon credits…but we don't have an operational licensing framework" — MWE official. The 2025 regulations aim to close this gap, but implementation capacity is still being built.

    ## NBS Resources — Switzerland Eligibility Tension

    > ⚠️ **Structural mismatch for NBS investors**: Uganda's carbon asset base is REDD+ and afforestation-dominant — precisely the category **blacklisted by Switzerland** (land use, forestry, biological CO₂ sequestration). The Switzerland-Uganda bilateral (Nov 2025) will therefore likely focus on **non-NBS project types** (cookstoves, renewable energy, transport efficiency). NBS/REDD+ project developers must target **other acquiring countries** (Singapore, Sweden, Norway via LEAF/ART — noting Norway's NOGER also excludes direct ITMO purchase of forestry) for Article 6.2 off-take.[^ugchexcl]

=== "🇯🇵 日本語"

    ## サマリー
    ウガンダは**東アフリカ最大の炭素クレジット保有国**。VCM（自主炭素市場）ポートフォリオは3,300万クレジット超で、NDCの82.7%はAFOLU（土地利用・林業・農業）セクターからの削減に依存。2025年4月に炭素市場規制（Climate Change Mechanisms Regulations 2025）を制定し、COP30（2025年11月）でスイスと二国間協定を締結。ただし、スイスが土地利用・林業クレジットをITMO除外としているため、ウガンダの主要炭素資産（REDD+）はスイス向けに販売不可という構造的なミスマッチが存在する。

    ## NBS投資家向け注記

    > ⚠️ **構造的ミスマッチ**: ウガンダの炭素資産の中核はREDD+・植林（スイスがITMOとして禁止するカテゴリー）。スイスとの二国間協定はクリーンクッキング・再エネ案件が中心となる見込み。NBS/REDD+開発者はシンガポール・スウェーデン等の他調達国を探索する必要があるが、現時点でウガンダとの二国間IAを持つ国はない。ノルウェーのNOGERも直接的な林業ITMOへの投資を除外していることに留意。
