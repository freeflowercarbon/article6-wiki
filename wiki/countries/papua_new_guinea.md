# Papua New Guinea 🇵🇬 | パプアニューギニア — Article 6.2 Host Country

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    Papua New Guinea (PNG) is one of the **most significant potential REDD+ host countries** globally — the world's 3rd largest tropical forest (outside Africa). PNG has **dual acquiring-country access**: Japan JCM (2014) and Singapore IA (2023). PNG was an early REDD+ pioneer but complex customary land tenure and governance challenges remain the key risk for A6.2 project development.

    ## Governance Challenges

    - **Customary land tenure**: ~97% of land is under customary ownership — project development requires complex, multi-stakeholder consent processes (FPIC)
    - **"Carbon cowboy" problem**: Foreign brokers have signed inequitable contracts with indigenous communities; CCDA tightened international deal authorization from 2022–2024
    - **CCDA strengthening**: International carbon transaction licensing now requires CCDA approval; integrity improved but approval timelines uncertain
    - **Transparency**: NDC registry and CA accounting infrastructure still under development (as of 2024)

    ## Investment Considerations

    - **Resource scale**: Potential credit volume among the largest in Asia-Pacific
    - **Dual access**: Japan JCM + Singapore IA is rare — multi-buyer optionality is valuable
    - **High risk**: Governance, land rights, political stability require experienced implementation partners
    - **Trusted partners key**: Organizations like WCS, Wild Melanesia with established community relationships are essential

    ## ガバナンス課題

    - **慣習的土地所有**: 土地の97%が慣習的所有権下。複雑な同意プロセス（FPIC）が必要
    - **「炭素カウボーイ」問題**: 外国業者による不適切な契約が社会問題化 → CCDAが2022〜2024年に許可制度を厳格化
    - **透明性**: NDC登録・CA記帳の整備は2024年時点でも進行中
