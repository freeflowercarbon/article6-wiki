# Azerbaijan 🇦🇿 | アゼルバイジャン — Article 6.2 Host Country

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    Azerbaijan is a Japan JCM partner (2016) and the **host of COP29 (Baku, November 2024)**. As a major oil and gas producer, Azerbaijan occupies a complex position in international climate negotiations. COP29 accelerated its domestic carbon market development commitments. The Baku Carbon Initiative launched at COP29 aims to support developing countries' carbon market capacity. Caspian region wind and solar offer the primary JCM opportunity.

    ## COP29 Impact

    - **Baku Carbon Initiative**: Azerbaijan-led initiative at COP29 to support developing countries' carbon market capacity building
    - **A6.4 mechanism finalized**: COP29 finalized Article 6.4 rules — Azerbaijan was the host
    - **Post-COP29 commitment**: Political commitment to accelerate domestic carbon market development

    ## Risk Factors

    - **Oil state ambivalence**: As an oil/gas exporter, political signals on carbon regulation are mixed
    - **Post-Nagorno-Karabakh stability**: 2023 military operation; regional stability affects investment climate

    日本JCM（2016年）パートナー。**COP29（2024年、バクー）開催国**として注目された石油・ガス産出国。COP29を契機に炭素市場整備への取り組みを宣言し、Baku Carbon Initiativeを主導。カスピ海周辺の風力・太陽光が主なJCM対象。

    ## COP29開催の影響

    - **Baku Carbon Initiative**: COP29でアゼルバイジャンが主導した途上国向け炭素市場開発支援
    - **A6.4メカニズム最終化**: COP29でArticle 6.4の詳細ルール合意
    - **ポストCOP29**: 炭素市場整備を加速する政治的コミットメントを確認

    ## 注意事項

    - **石油国の複雑な立場**: 石油・ガス輸出国として炭素規制にアンビバレントな姿勢
    - **ナゴルノ・カラバフ後の安定化**: 2023年の軍事作戦後の地域安定化が投資環境に影響
