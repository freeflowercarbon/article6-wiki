# Norway 🇳🇴 | ノルウェー — Article 6.2 Acquiring Country

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    Norway is an active Article 6.2 acquiring country, primarily channeling demand through bilateral carbon credit agreements. Norway signed a bilateral agreement with **Zambia at COP29 (November 2024)**.[^noza] Norway also contributes to international climate finance through the **Norway International Climate and Forests Initiative (NICFI)** — though NICFI is results-based grant finance, not Article 6.2 credit purchasing. Norway has an EU ETS-linked domestic carbon market.

    ## Carbon Pricing System

    Norway operates a **hybrid system**: domestic CO₂ tax (NOK 1,125/t ≈ US$105, among the world's highest) + Norwegian ETS (linked to EU ETS). The ETS covers large emitters (power, industry); the CO₂ tax covers non-ETS sectors (transport, buildings). Norway aims to reach NOK 2,000/t by 2030 (CO₂ tax escalation).[^noco2]

    The domestic carbon price is very high by global standards, meaning Norway has strong economic incentive to seek lower-cost international reductions through A6.2 while maintaining domestic ambition.

    ノルウェーはA6.2調達国として、**COP29（2024年11月）でザンビアとの二国間協定を署名**[^noza]。国内ではEU ETSにリンクしたノルウェーETSとCO₂税（NOK 1,125/t≈US$105、世界最高水準の一つ）を運用。NICFIによる森林保全グラント資金提供（A6.2クレジット購入とは別系統）でも国際的に知名度が高い。

    ## 炭素価格制度の概要

    ノルウェーは**ハイブリッド型制度**：大規模排出者向けETS（EU ETSリンク）と、非ETS部門向けCO₂税（2030年までにNOK 2,000/tへ引き上げ計画）。国内価格が世界最高水準のため、A6.2経由のコスト効率的な国際削減調達に強い経済的インセンティブがある。
