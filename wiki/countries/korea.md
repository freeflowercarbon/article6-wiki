# Korea 🇰🇷 | 韓国 — Article 6.2 Acquiring Country

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    Korea operates **Asia's largest cap-and-trade system** (K-ETS, since 2015) and is positioning as a future Article 6.2 acquiring country. Korea's 2030 NDC (40% reduction vs. 2018) creates structural demand for international credits, particularly as Phase 4 (2026–2030) design includes expanded A6.2-compatible credit acceptance. No bilateral A6.2 agreements have been signed to date, but informal discussions with ASEAN partners are ongoing.[^krndc]

    | Item | Detail |
    |------|--------|
    | NDC Target | 40% reduction vs. 2018 by 2030; net-zero 2050 |
    | Carbon System | K-ETS (Korea Emissions Trading Scheme, 2015–present) |
    | K-ETS Price (2024–25) | KRW 8,000–18,000/t (≈ US$6–13) |
    | K-ETS Price (2030 est.) | KRW 20,000–40,000/t (≈ US$15–29) |
    | International Credit Limit | 5% of allocation (K-ETS Phase 3) |
    | Key Ministry | Ministry of Environment (MOE); Ministry of Foreign Affairs |
    | A6.2 Role | Potential acquiring country (framework under development) |

    ## Carbon Pricing System

    Korea's **K-ETS** is the largest carbon trading system in Asia (outside China's national ETS). Operating since 2015, it covers ~685 companies representing ~73% of national GHG emissions.

    | Phase | Period | Price Range | International Credit Rules |
    |-------|--------|-------------|--------------------------|
    | Phase 1 | 2015–2017 | KRW 8,000–11,000/t | None permitted |
    | Phase 2 | 2018–2020 | KRW 10,000–25,000/t | CDM credits ≤10% |
    | Phase 3 | 2021–2025 | KRW 8,000–18,000/t | External credits ≤5% (mostly CDM) |
    | Phase 4 | 2026–2030 | Est. KRW 20,000–40,000/t | A6.2-compatible credits expected [^krpha4] |

    Korea's domestic carbon price is well below Switzerland or Sweden (US$6–13 vs. US$105–133), limiting procurement price capacity. However, K-ETS Phase 4 price escalation is expected as free allocation decreases, which will make international A6.2 credits more economically attractive for Korean covered entities.

    ## Procurement Track Record & Demand

    | Metric | Data |
    |--------|------|
    | Active A6.2 bilateral agreements | 0 (as of May 2026) |
    | KCU usage (Phase 3) | Limited; low price has reduced incentive |
    | Phase 4 demand outlook | Significant — if alignment is finalized, 685 covered firms face increasing obligations |
    | ASEAN cooperation | ACMC (ASEAN Carbon Market Cooperation) context; Korea is an observer |

    **Demand outlook**: Korea's potential demand for A6.2 credits is large in absolute terms (covered emissions ~600 Mt CO₂e/yr) but remains contingent on Phase 4 design decisions. If KCU eligibility is extended to A6.2 ITMOs with a 5%+ cap and price reaches KRW 30,000–40,000/t (≈ US$22–29), Korea would become a significant buyer — particularly from ASEAN JCM countries. The 2025–2026 period is critical for policy finalization.

    ## 炭素価格制度の概要

    **K-ETS**はアジア最大の国内キャップ＆トレード制度（中国ETS除く）。2015年開始、約685社（国家GHG排出量の約73%をカバー）。フェーズ3（2021〜2025年）の炭素価格はKRW 8,000〜18,000/t（≈US$6〜13）と低水準。フェーズ4（2026〜2030）では無償割当削減に伴い価格上昇を見込む。A6.2適合クレジットの受入が認められれば、ASEAN JCM国からの調達が経済合理的になる。

    ## 調達実績・需要見通し

    - 現時点でA6.2二国間協定はゼロ
    - フェーズ3のKCU使用は限定的（低価格のため調達インセンティブ薄）
    - フェーズ4の設計次第で大きな調達需要が発生する潜在力あり
    - カバード排出量≈6億t/yr × 5%上限 = 年間最大3,000万t規模の潜在需要
