# CBAM × Article 6.2 Impact
# CBAMがArticle 6.2市場に与える影響

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    The EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) entered its **definitive phase on January 1, 2026** (Q1 2026 certificate price: **€75.36/t**[^cbamq1]). CBAM creates a new structural demand layer for Article 6.2 credits through a two-tier carbon price deduction framework — but the interaction with Article 6 credits is indirect, capped, and not yet fully settled in EU law.

    | Item | Detail |
    |------|--------|
    | CBAM definitive phase | January 1, 2026 |
    | Q1 2026 certificate price | **€75.36/t CO₂e** (linked to EU ETS quarterly average)[^cbamq1] |
    | Covered sectors (Phase 1) | Steel, aluminium, cement, fertilisers, hydrogen |
    | Phase 2 expansion | 2028: 180 downstream products (steel/aluminium-intensive)[^cbamiext] |
    | Aug 2025 | Call for evidence — CBAM emissions methodology & carbon pricing rules[^cbamcfe] |
    | Oct 2025 | Amended CBAM Regulation (simplification) published in Official Journal[^cbamamd] |
    | Dec 2025 | Commission proposal — strengthened anti-circumvention provisions[^cbamcirc] |
    | May 13 – Jun 10, 2026 | Public consultation on **Implementing Act for third-country carbon price recognition** (Tier 1 + Tier 2 Article 6 credits)[^cbamcons] |
    | Article 6 credits (Tier 2) | **Accepted up to 10% of reported embedded emissions** per Implementing Act draft[^tfg] |
    | Key uncertainty | "Transitivity" — whether Singapore's carbon tax (which allows 5% A6 offset) is automatically CBAM-recognised as Tier 1 |

    ## Two-Tier Carbon Price Deduction Structure

    CBAM allows importers to deduct carbon prices already paid in the country of production. The critical distinction is between two tiers:

    | | Tier 1: Domestic Carbon Price | Tier 2: Article 6 Credits |
    |---|---|---|
    | **Examples** | National ETS, carbon tax (e.g. Singapore S$45/t, Japan GX-ETS) | ITMO (Art. 6.2), 6.4ER (Art. 6.4) |
    | **Deduction** | **Full deduction** — no EU quality screening | **Up to 10% cap** on reported embedded emissions |
    | **EU quality check** | None (proof of payment only) | "Real, additional, measurable, independently verified" |
    | **Status** | Established | Draft rule (May 13, 2026 — public comment period)[^tfg] |

    **Arbitrage logic**: CBAM certificate price (€75/t) × 10% cap means EU importers can save up to **€7.54/t** by substituting Article 6 credits (if priced below €75/t). This creates structural demand.

    ## Singapore's Argument — The Transitivity Problem

    Singapore advocates that its carbon tax payments should qualify A6 credits for CBAM recognition via two pathways:

    **Path A (Direct — Tier 1)**
    Singapore exporters pay carbon tax (S$45/t ≈ €31/t) → claim as Tier 1 CBAM deduction. Gap: S$45/t << €75.36/t certificate price → only partial offset.

    **Path B (Indirect — "Transitivity")**
    Singapore carbon tax (Tier 1, full deduction) → 5% of that tax liability can be paid with A6 ICCs → EU recognises the carbon tax as Tier 1 → A6 credits are **indirectly** embedded in the CBAM-recognised carbon price.

    > "If the EU recognizes the Singaporean carbon tax as an Equivalent Carbon Price, by default, the EU will also automatically recognize the use of Article 6 credits."[^belfer]

    [^belfer]: Belfer Center for Science and International Affairs: <https://www.belfercenter.org/research-analysis/cbam-climate-finance>

    The EU has **not explicitly resolved** the transitivity issue as of May 2026.

    ## Price Implications for Article 6.2 Credits

    | Scenario | Assumption | A6.2 Price Impact |
    |----------|------------|-------------------|
    | **Limited recognition** | EU keeps Tier 2 10% cap; strict quality requirements | Modest demand increase; **$5–15/t premium** for high-integrity A6 (CCP, ART TREES) |
    | **Partial recognition** | Singapore carbon tax Tier 1 with transitivity for 5% | Additional Singapore-IA-country demand premium |
    | **Full recognition** | A6 credits treated Tier 1-equivalent | Price convergence toward EU ETS (€75/t) — multi-fold increase from current $5–30/t |

    Most likely near-term scenario (May 2026): **Limited recognition**. Full transitivity recognition is inconsistent with stated EU policy intent.

    **Multi-market demand stack**: High-integrity A6.2 credits now face simultaneous demand from CBAM (10%), GX-ETS (10%), Singapore carbon tax (5%), and Korea K-ETS (5%), each with Article 6.2 authorization requirements — creating structural price floor conditions for authorized credits.

    ## Japan's CBAM Exposure

    | Sector | EU Export Volume | CBAM Risk | Key Factor |
    |--------|-----------------|-----------|------------|
    | Steel (Nippon Steel, JFE) | Moderate direct EU exposure | Medium | GX-ETS "EU equivalent" recognition outcome |
    | Aluminium | Limited | Low | — |
    | Cement | Negligible EU exports | Very Low | — |
    | **Competitive impact** | Korean/Indian exporters same CBAM | High indirect | If GX-ETS not EU-equivalent, Japan at competitive disadvantage vs. EU-ETS-linked economies |

    **GX-ETS EU-equivalence**: If recognised, full CBAM deduction applies. If not, only GX-ETS price (¥4,300 ≈ €28/t) deductible vs. certificate price of €75/t — leaving a €47/t gap.

=== "🇯🇵 日本語"

    ## 概要

    EU CBAM（炭素国境調整メカニズム）は2026年1月1日に定常フェーズへ移行（Q1 2026証書価格：**€75.36/t**）。CBAMはArticle 6.2クレジットへの新たな需要層を生み出すが、その相互作用は間接的・上限付きであり、EU法としての最終確定はまだ行われていない。

    ## 2層控除構造

    CBAM核心ロジック：輸入品の炭素コストのうち、原産国ですでに支払った炭素価格分を控除できる。

    | | Tier 1（国内炭素価格） | Tier 2（Article 6クレジット） |
    |---|---|---|
    | 対象 | ETS・炭素税（Singapore S$45/t、Japan GX-ETS等） | ITMO（A6.2）、6.4ER（A6.4） |
    | 控除 | **全額控除**（品質審査なし） | **10%上限**（2026年5月13日EU草案） |
    | 品質要件 | なし（支払い証明のみ） | 「real, additional, measurable, verified」 |

    ## Singaporeの議論：Transitivity問題

    Singaporeは炭素税（S$45/t）のうち5%をA6 ICCで充当できる制度を持つ。EUがSingapore炭素税をTier 1として認定した場合、その5%分（A6クレジット）が間接的にCBAM控除に「乗る」という構造（推移性問題）。EUは2026年5月時点で明示的に解決していない。

    ## A6.2クレジット価格への含意

    | シナリオ | A6.2価格への影響 |
    |---------|----------------|
    | 限定認定（最蓋然） | 高インテグリティA6に$5〜15/tプレミアム形成 |
    | 部分認定（Transitivity一部） | Singapore IA国クレジットへの追加プレミアム |
    | 完全認定（Transitivity完全） | EU ETS価格（€75/t）に向けた価格上昇（現状の数倍） |

    **需要の多層化**：CBAM（10%）・GX-ETS（10%）・Singapore炭素税（5%）・K-ETS（5%）が同時にA6.2認証クレジットを需要する構造が生まれ、高品質クレジットへの価格フロア形成圧力が高まっている。

    ## 日本への含意

    - **直接エクスポージャー**：日本のEU向け鉄鋼・アルミ輸出はそれほど大きくないため、CBAM直接コストは限定的（RIETI試算）
    - **競争条件**：GX-ETSがEU ETSと「同等認定」されるかどうかが競争力の鍵。認定されれば全額控除、されなければ日本輸出企業が韓国・インド対比で不利になる
    - **JCMクレジット**：ITMO（A6.2 JCM）はCBAM Tier 2の「Article 6.2 authorized ITMO」に原則該当。高インテグリティ要件（CCP相当か？）の定義が今後の焦点
