# Reversal Risk — 永続性・リバーサルリスクの整理

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    **Reversal risk** (also called non-permanence risk) refers to the possibility that carbon sequestered or avoided by a project is later released back to the atmosphere — for example, when a protected forest burns, is illegally logged, or when a project collapses. Under Article 6.2, reversal events can undermine the integrity of ITMOs already transferred and used in acquiring countries' NDC accounting.

    **The core policy question**: *If a reversal occurs and exceeds the buffer, who bears the obligation to replace the lost carbon?*

    ## How Different Systems Handle Reversal Risk

    ### 1. JCM (Japan Bilateral Credit Mechanism)

    | Element | JCM Approach |
    |---------|-------------|
    | Buffer pool requirement | Methodology-specific (typically 5–15% set-aside) |
    | NBS-specific rules | Limited; JCM has historically focused on technology projects |
    | Published policy on post-buffer reversal | **Not published** (as of 2026) |
    | Who bears obligation if buffer exceeded | Host country government (as authorizing party) |
    | Acquirer's NDC treatment | ITMOs already transferred are NOT recalled — host country absorbs the gap |
    | Key risk for investors | If host government cannot provide replacement ITMOs, the ecological and financial loss falls on the project developer |

    **Practical implication**: Japan MOEJ has not published an explicit "make-good" framework for JCM NBS reversals. This creates regulatory uncertainty for large-scale NBS JCM projects. Investors should verify project-level buffer adequacy with GEC (Global Environment Centre Foundation).

    > Source: JCM Methodology Framework — <https://gec.jp/jcm/projects/> | MOEJ JCM Portal — <https://www.env.go.jp/earth/ondanka/jcm_platform.html>

    ---

    ### 2. Singapore Implementation Agreement (IA) System

    | Element | Singapore IA Approach |
    |---------|----------------------|
    | Buffer pool requirement | Required; project-specific risk assessment by NEA |
    | Eligibility criteria | Include permanence and reversal risk management as explicit standards |
    | Published policy on post-buffer reversal | **Partially published** in NEA eligibility criteria |
    | Who bears obligation if buffer exceeded | Host country government must provide replacement ITMOs |
    | Acquirer's NDC treatment | Singapore retains already-transferred ITMOs; host country is responsible for replacement |
    | Key safeguard | NEA requires multi-year monitoring and reporting; project must demonstrate risk management |

    **Practical implication**: Singapore's NEA eligibility criteria are the most explicit of any A6.2 buyer on permanence requirements. However, the exact threshold at which the host government's obligation is triggered (buffer amount, grace period) is not fully public. Investors should review NEA's published eligibility criteria for each IA.

    > Source: NEA International Carbon Credits — <https://www.nea.gov.sg/our-services/climate-change-energy-efficiency/singapore-s-international-climate-change-cooperation/international-cooperation-on-climate-change>

    ---

    ### 3. Switzerland FOEN Bilateral Agreements

    | Element | FOEN Approach |
    |---------|--------------|
    | Buffer pool requirement | Required; project-specific |
    | Published policy | Bilateral agreements include permanence clauses |
    | Who bears obligation if buffer exceeded | Host country government |
    | Acquirer's NDC treatment | Switzerland retains ITMOs; host country replaces |
    | Notable | World's first A6.2 transaction (Switzerland-Peru, 2023) set precedent |

    > Source: Swiss FOEN Bilateral Agreements — <https://www.bafu.admin.ch/bafu/en/home/topics/climate/info-specialists/climate-and-climate-change/policy/the-paris-agreement/bilateral-agreements-on-climate-change.html>

    ---

    ### 4. Voluntary Market Standards (Verra / Gold Standard)

    | Standard | Buffer Pool | Who Manages | Post-Exhaustion Rule |
    |----------|-------------|-------------|---------------------|
    | Verra VCS (AFOLU) | AFOLU Pooled Buffer (~30–60 yr horizon) | Verra centrally manages | If pooled buffer exhausted, credits become non-tradeable |
    | Gold Standard | Project-level buffer | Project developer | Replacement required by developer |
    | ART TREES | Jurisdictional buffer pool | National government | Government replaces |

    **CCP (ICVCM) and reversal**: ICVCM's Core Carbon Principles require robust permanence risk management as a prerequisite. Projects must demonstrate adequate buffer pools AND monitoring protocols. CCP approval does NOT eliminate reversal risk but certifies that the risk management system meets minimum standards.

    > Source: ICVCM Core Carbon Principles — <https://icvcm.org/the-core-carbon-principles/>

    ---

    ## Systemic Risk: The Accountability Gap

    A key unresolved issue in A6.2:

    1. A host country authorizes ITMOs and transfers them to Japan/Singapore
    2. Japan/Singapore uses them in NDC accounting — "books" the emission reductions
    3. A major reversal occurs (fire, deforestation, project failure) exceeding the buffer
    4. Host country is obligated to provide replacement ITMOs — but has NO budget or ready supply
    5. **Result**: Acquiring country's NDC accounting shows reductions that no longer exist in reality

    **Current status**: No A6.2 agreement has published an explicit enforcement mechanism for this scenario. This remains an open systemic risk in the Article 6.2 framework, and a key argument for why high-quality, monitored NBS projects with robust buffer pools are essential.

    ## Investor Guidance

    | Risk Level | Project Type | Recommendation |
    |------------|-------------|----------------|
    | Low | Technology projects (solar, efficiency) — no reversal risk | Standard due diligence |
    | Medium | Mangrove, grassland — shorter-duration NBS | Verify buffer size; seek host govt commitment |
    | High | Tropical REDD+ in politically unstable areas | Require large buffer (>20%); independent monitoring; government guarantee |
    | Critical | Large-scale REDD+ with weak governance | Avoid until A6.2 framework clarifies accountability |

=== "🇯🇵 日本語"

    ## 概要

    **リバーサルリスク（永続性リスク）**とは、プロジェクトが固定・削減した炭素が後に大気中に再放出されるリスク。森林火災・違法伐採・プロジェクト崩壊などが原因となりうる。Article 6.2においては、既に移転したITMOに対するNDC会計の信頼性を根本的に揺るがす問題。

    **核心的な政策問題**：*リバーサルがバッファを超えた場合、誰が失われた炭素の補填義務を負うのか？*

    ## 各制度のリバーサルリスク対処

    ### 1. JCM（日本二国間クレジット制度）

    | 項目 | JCMの対応 |
    |------|----------|
    | バッファプール要件 | 方法論別（通常5〜15%積み立て） |
    | NBS固有のルール | 限定的（JCMは技術型プロジェクト中心） |
    | バッファ超過後の補填義務 | **未公表**（2026年時点） |
    | 補填義務の帰属先 | ホスト国政府（ITMOを授権した主体として） |
    | 調達国のNDC扱い | 既に移転したITMOは回収されない → ホスト国がギャップを吸収 |
    | 投資家へのリスク | ホスト国政府が補填ITMOを調達できない場合、損失は事業者に帰属 |

    **実務上の含意**：環境省はJCM NBS案件のリバーサルに対する明示的な「補填義務」枠組みを公表していない。大規模NBS-JCM投資においては、GEC（地球環境センター）に方法論レベルのバッファ条件を確認することが重要。

    ---

    ### 2. シンガポールIA制度

    | 項目 | シンガポールIAの対応 |
    |------|-------------------|
    | バッファプール要件 | 必須（NEAがリスク評価）|
    | 適格基準 | 永続性・リバーサルリスク管理を明示的に要求 |
    | バッファ超過後の方針 | 一部公表済み（NEA適格基準に記載） |
    | 補填義務の帰属先 | ホスト国政府が代替ITMOを提供する義務 |
    | シンガポールのNDC扱い | 既に移転したITMOは保持。ホスト国が補填責任 |
    | 主要な保護措置 | NEAが多年間モニタリング・報告を義務付け |

    **実務上の含意**：シンガポールは調達国の中でも永続性要件が最も明確。ただし、ホスト国の義務が発動する具体的な閾値（バッファ量・猶予期間等）は完全には公開されていない。各IAの適格基準を確認することが必要。

    ---

    ### 3. ボランタリー市場（Verra/Gold Standard）

    | 規格 | バッファプール | 管理主体 | バッファ枯渇後 |
    |------|------------|---------|-------------|
    | Verra VCS (AFOLU) | AFOLU共同プール | Verra中央管理 | プールが枯渇したら取引不可 |
    | Gold Standard | プロジェクト個別 | 事業者 | 事業者が補填 |
    | ART TREES | 国家管轄バッファ | 国家政府 | 政府が補填 |

    ---

    ## システミックリスク：責任の空白

    Article 6.2に残る未解決の重大問題：

    1. ホスト国がITMOを授権・移転（→ 日本/シンガポールがNDC会計に計上）
    2. 大規模リバーサル発生（バッファを超過）
    3. ホスト国が代替ITMOを提供する義務を負う
    4. しかし予算・代替供給源を持たない場合、補填不能に
    5. **結果**：調達国のNDC会計が実態のない排出削減を計上し続ける

    現時点でこのシナリオに対する強制執行メカニズムを明示したA6.2協定は存在せず、Article 6.2フレームワーク全体の未解決リスクとして残る。

    ## 投資家ガイダンス

    | リスクレベル | プロジェクト種別 | 推奨事項 |
    |------------|--------------|---------|
    | 低 | 太陽光・省エネ等技術型（リバーサルなし） | 標準的DDで十分 |
    | 中 | マングローブ・草地等中期NBS | バッファサイズ確認・ホスト国コミットメント取得 |
    | 高 | 政情不安地域の熱帯REDD+ | 大バッファ（>20%）・独立モニタリング・政府保証を要求 |
    | 重大 | ガバナンス脆弱な大規模REDD+ | A6.2責任枠組み明確化まで避けることを推奨 |
