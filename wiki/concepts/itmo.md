# ITMO — Internationally Transferred Mitigation Outcome

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Definition

    An **ITMO (Internationally Transferred Mitigation Outcome)** is the unit of account for international carbon credit transfers under **Article 6.2** of the Paris Agreement. When Country A achieves greenhouse gas emission reductions, those reductions can be transferred to Country B and used toward Country B's Nationally Determined Contribution (NDC) — but only if both countries apply the required accounting rules.

    ITMOs are not a specific format like a "carbon credit" — they are an accounting concept. The underlying emission reduction may be measured in tonnes of CO₂ equivalent (tCO₂e) but can also cover other metrics depending on bilateral agreement.

    ## Requirements for a Valid ITMO

    1. **Bilateral Agreement (Implementation Agreement / IA)**: The host country and acquiring country must have a formal Article 6.2 IA or equivalent bilateral agreement in place.
    2. **Authorization**: The host country government must formally authorize the specific project and the ITMO transfer (written letter of authorization or equivalent).
    3. **Corresponding Adjustment (CA)**: The host country must deduct the transferred volume from its own NDC accounting ("subtract from the host's balance").
    4. **Reporting**: Both countries must report to the UNFCCC in the prescribed format (biennial transparency reports, Article 6 transparency framework).
    5. **Robust MRV**: Measurement, reporting, and verification of emission reductions to an acceptable standard (varies by acquiring country — Switzerland accepts Verra/GS; JCM uses proprietary Joint Committee methodologies).

    ## ITMOs vs. Voluntary Carbon Credits

    | Criterion | ITMO (A6.2) | Voluntary Carbon Credit (VCM) |
    |-----------|-------------|-------------------------------|
    | Legal basis | Paris Agreement Article 6.2 | Private standard (Verra, GS, etc.) |
    | CA applied | Yes (mandatory) | No (unless specifically required) |
    | Government authorization | Required | Not required |
    | UNFCCC reporting | Mandatory | Not required |
    | Double-counting risk | Eliminated by CA | Present (unless CA applied) |
    | Use for NDC compliance | Yes (acquiring country) | Only if government accepts |
    | Quality premium | High | Variable |

    > **Key point**: A voluntary carbon credit (e.g., VCS-certified) does NOT automatically become an ITMO. It requires government authorization + CA to be valid for NDC compliance use.

    ## ITMOs vs. A6.4ER (PACM)

    | Criterion | ITMO (A6.2) | A6.4ER (PACM / Article 6.4) |
    |-----------|-------------|------------------------------|
    | Governance | Bilateral governments | UNFCCC A6.4 Supervisory Body |
    | Methodology approval | Bilateral Joint Committee | UNFCCC A6.4SB centrally |
    | CA requirement | Yes | Yes (Type B only) |
    | Registry | Host country + bilateral | UNFCCC centralized |
    | NBS/REDD+ approval | Bilateral (active) | Pending A6.4SB methodology review |
    | Current status (2026) | Active — ITMOs already issued | Pipeline forming |

    ## JCM Credits and ITMO Status

    JCM credits issued by Japan's bilateral Joint Committees were **not automatically ITMOs** until:
    - March 2025: Japan officially recognized JCM credits as Article 6.2-compatible
    - JCM credits now require CA to be used as ITMOs (transition processing ongoing as of 2026)

    | Item | JCM Credit (pre-2025) | JCM Credit (post-March 2025) |
    |------|----------------------|------------------------------|
    | A6.2 status | Bilateral only | ✅ Article 6.2 compatible |
    | CA required | Not mandatory | Mandatory for ITMO use |
    | UNFCCC reporting | Voluntary | Required for ITMO transfers |

=== "🇯🇵 日本語"

    ## 定義

    **ITMO（国際移転緩和成果）**は、パリ協定**Article 6.2**の枠組みで国際的に移転される炭素削減量の会計単位。A国が達成した温室効果ガス削減量をB国が取得し、B国のNDC達成に使用できる。ただし、双方が定められた会計ルール（Corresponding Adjustment）を適用することが必須条件。

    ITMOは「炭素クレジット」のような物理的な証書ではなく、会計上の概念。基礎となる削減量はtCO₂e単位で計測されるが、二国間合意次第で他の指標も可。

    ## 有効なITMOの成立要件

    1. **二国間協定（IA）の存在**: ホスト国と調達国がArticle 6.2のIA（実施協定）または同等の二国間協定を締結
    2. **授権（Authorization）**: ホスト国政府が当該プロジェクトとITMO移転を公式に承認（書面による授権書等）
    3. **Corresponding Adjustment（CA）**: ホスト国が移転量を自国NDCの会計から控除（二重計上の防止）
    4. **報告義務**: 両国がUNFCCCに規定フォーマットで報告（隔年透明性報告書、Article 6透明性フレームワーク）
    5. **強固なMRV**: 削減量の測定・報告・検証（調達国ごとに基準が異なる：スイスはVerra/GS承認、JCMは独自Joint Committee方法論）

    ## ITMOとボランタリークレジットの違い

    | 比較軸 | ITMO（A6.2） | ボランタリークレジット（VCM） |
    |--------|-------------|--------------------------|
    | 法的根拠 | パリ協定Article 6.2 | 民間規格（Verra・GS等） |
    | CA適用 | 必須 | 原則なし |
    | 政府授権 | 必要 | 不要 |
    | UNFCCC報告 | 義務 | 不要 |
    | 二重計上リスク | CAで排除 | あり（CA適用なければ） |
    | NDC充当 | 可能（調達国） | 政府が受け入れる場合のみ |
    | 品質プレミアム | 高 | 変動 |

    > **重要**: VoluntaryクレジットはそのままではITMOにならない。政府授権+CAがあって初めてNDC充当に使えるITMOとなる。

    ## JCMクレジットとITMOの関係

    JCMクレジットは**2025年3月まで自動的にはITMOではなかった**：
    - **2025年3月**: 日本がJCMクレジットのArticle 6.2適合を正式認定
    - 現在、既存JCMクレジットのITMO化処理が進行中（CA付与が必要）

    | 項目 | JCMクレジット（2025年3月以前） | JCMクレジット（2025年3月以降） |
    |------|------------------------------|------------------------------|
    | A6.2ステータス | 二国間のみ | ✅ Article 6.2適合 |
    | CA要否 | 任意 | ITMO使用には必須 |
    | UNFCCC報告 | 任意 | ITMO移転には必須 |
