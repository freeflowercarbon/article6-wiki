# PACM — Paris Agreement Crediting Mechanism (Article 6.4)

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    The **Paris Agreement Crediting Mechanism (PACM)**, established under **Article 6.4** of the Paris Agreement, is the successor to the Clean Development Mechanism (CDM). It creates a UNFCCC-supervised multilateral crediting system where emission reductions are certified and traded internationally under centralized governance — unlike Article 6.2's bilateral approach.

    **Key distinction from Article 6.2**: Under PACM, the UNFCCC A6.4 Supervisory Body certifies methodologies and credits centrally. Under Article 6.2, two governments bilaterally authorize ITMOs using their own Joint Committee. PACM provides more standardization but less flexibility; A6.2 is faster and more flexible but requires country-by-country negotiation.

    ## Governance Structure

    | Element | Detail |
    |---------|--------|
    | Legal Basis | Article 6.4, Paris Agreement |
    | Supervisory Body | Article 6.4 Supervisory Body (A6.4SB) |
    | Established | COP26, Glasgow, November 2021 |
    | Methodological framework adopted | A6.4SB 10th Meeting, October 2024 |
    | Full operationalization | COP29, Baku, November 2024 |
    | Registry | UNFCCC centralized registry (in development; launch targeted 2026) |
    | Unit Name | Article 6.4 Emission Reduction (A6.4ER) |

    ## Two Types of A6.4 Units

    | Type | Name | Use | CA Required |
    |------|------|-----|-------------|
    | Type A | Mitigation Contribution (MC) | Host country NDC accounting only | No |
    | Type B | ITMO-eligible A6.4ER | International transfer; acquiring country NDC use | Yes |

    Only Type B units (with Corresponding Adjustment) can be used by acquiring countries to meet their NDCs. Type A units contribute to the host country's NDC only.

    ## Comparison: PACM vs. CDM vs. Article 6.2

    | Criterion | CDM (legacy) | PACM (A6.4) | Article 6.2 |
    |-----------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
    | Governance | UNFCCC Executive Board | UNFCCC A6.4SB | Bilateral governments |
    | Methodology approval | UNFCCC EB | A6.4SB | Bilateral Joint Committee |
    | Additionality standard | Conservative (project-level) | Enhanced (updated baselines) | Bilateral agreement |
    | CA requirement | No | Yes (Type B only) | Yes |
    | Registry | CDM registry (winding down) | UNFCCC central registry | Host country + bilateral |
    | Speed to registration | 18–36 months | Uncertain (target <18 months) | Faster (bilateral) |
    | Status (Q2 2026) | Wind-down; new registrations closed | **First credits issued Feb 26, 2026** (Myanmar, 58,428 t) | Active; ITMOs issued |
    | NBS / REDD+ | Widely used (but no CA) | Under methodology review | Used bilaterally (JCM, SG IA) |

    ## CDM to PACM Transition

    - CDM projects may apply to transition to PACM by the specified deadline (extended to Dec 2025)
    - Transition requirements: updated baseline (typically 10-year refresh), enhanced additionality, host country re-authorization, A6.4SB approval
    - **Most legacy CDM projects face barriers**: newer additionality standards disqualify older fossil-fuel efficiency projects; many large REDD+ projects cannot easily adapt to PACM's permanence requirements
    - CDM credits already issued: some are CORSIA-eligible (vintage 2016+) but do NOT automatically transfer to PACM — they remain as CDM credits
    - Estimate: ~10–20% of active CDM projects may successfully transition to PACM [^cdmtrans]

    ## PACM vs. Article 6.2 — Which Track for NBS?

    For Nature-Based Solutions (forest, peatland, blue carbon):

    | Consideration | PACM (A6.4) | Article 6.2 |
    |--------------|-------------|-------------|
    | REDD+ methodology approved? | 🔴 Not yet | 🟡 Bilateral JC approval (JCM) / Verra VCS (SG, CH) |
    | Permanence buffer requirement | Strict (buffer pool mandatory) | Bilateral negotiation |
    | Speed | Slower (A6.4SB bottleneck) | Faster (bilateral) |
    | Market demand | Uncertain (no CORSIA link yet) | Confirmed demand (Switzerland, Singapore, Japan) |
    | Recommended track (2025–2027) | 🟡 Wait for methodology approval | ✅ Active track for immediate action |

    **Practical implication**: For project developers seeking Article 6 credits in 2025–2027, Article 6.2 bilateral channels (JCM, Singapore IA, Switzerland FOEN) remain more actionable than PACM. PACM will become more important post-2027 once methodologies are approved and the registry is operational.

=== "🇯🇵 日本語"

    ## 概要

    **PACM（パリ協定クレジット制度）**は、パリ協定**Article 6.4**に基づくCDM（クリーン開発メカニズム）の後継制度。UNFCCC A6.4監督機関（A6.4SB）が方法論とクレジットを中央集権的に認証する多国間の仕組みで、二国間合意で運営されるArticle 6.2とは対照的。

    **Article 6.2との違い**: PACMはUNFCCCの監督機関が一元認証。Article 6.2は二国間政府が各自のJoint CommitteeでITMOを授権。PACMの方が標準化されているが、柔軟性は低い。Article 6.2は二国間交渉なので迅速だが、国ごとの交渉が必要。

    ## 2種類のA6.4ユニット

    | 種類 | 名称 | 用途 | CA必要 |
    |------|------|------|--------|
    | Type A | 緩和貢献クレジット（MC） | ホスト国NDCのみ | 不要 |
    | Type B | ITMO適格A6.4ER | 国際移転・調達国NDC充当 | 必要 |

    国際移転・調達国のNDC充当に使えるのはType B（CA付き）のみ。

    ## CDMからPACMへの移行

    - CDMプロジェクトは期限までにPACM移行申請が可能（2025年12月まで延長）
    - 移行条件：ベースライン更新（通常10年更新）、追加性の強化、ホスト国再授権、A6.4SB承認
    - **ほとんどのCDMプロジェクトは移行困難**：古い化石燃料効率化プロジェクトは新追加性基準をクリアできず。REDD+は恒久性要件への対応が難しい
    - 移行成功見込み：稼働中CDMプロジェクトの約10〜20%と推定[^cdmtrans]

    ## NBS（自然に基づく解決策）ではどちらを選ぶか

    | 考慮点 | PACM（A6.4） | Article 6.2 |
    |--------|-------------|-------------|
    | REDD+方法論の承認 | 🔴 未承認 | 🟡 JCM：JC承認済み / SG・CH：Verra VCS活用 |
    | 恒久性バッファー | 厳格（バッファープール必須） | 二国間交渉による |
    | 登録スピード | 遅い（A6.4SBがボトルネック） | 速い（二国間） |
    | 市場需要 | 未確定（CORSIAリンクなし） | 確定（スイス・シンガポール・日本） |
    | 2025〜2027の推奨 | 🟡 方法論承認待ち | ✅ 即戦力 |

    **実務的結論**: 2025〜2027年においては、Article 6.2の二国間チャネル（JCM・SG IA・スイスFOEN）の方が、PACM待ちより実行可能性が高い。PACM本格活用は方法論承認・登録簿稼働後（2027年以降）が現実的。
