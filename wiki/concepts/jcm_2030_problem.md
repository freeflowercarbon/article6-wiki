# JCM 2030 Problem | JCM 2030年問題

=== "🇬🇧 English"

    ## Overview

    The **JCM 2030 Problem** is not a single issue but a **complex of six structural risks** arising from the misalignment between JCM credit issuance, Corresponding Adjustment (CA) acquisition, and NDC accounting timelines. Private-sector investors face maximum risk because they must commit capital before the regulatory pathways for 2030+ credit use are fully settled.

    Japan's 2025 NDC update (FY2035: 60% reduction vs. FY2013) provides a partial bridge — but the transition period (2030–2035) remains a regulatory gap for project developers.

    | Item | Detail |
    |------|--------|
    | JCM 2030 cumulative target | **100 Mt CO₂e** (all partner countries) |
    | Issued through March 2026 | **817,247 t CO₂e** (≈ 0.82 Mt) |
    | Target attainment rate | **~0.82%** |
    | Annual volume required (simple avg.) | **~22 Mt/yr** — 27× current pace |
    | Japan 2035 NDC | FY2035: 60% reduction vs. FY2013 (submitted 2025) |
    | JCMA established | April 2025 (centralised CA management) |

    > Sources: IETA "Carbon Markets & Emissions Trading in Japan" (May 2026); JCM Implementation Agency (JCMA) 2026

    ## Article 6.2 Legal Sequence

    COP21 Glasgow Rulebook (2021) + COP29 Decision 4/CMA.6 (2024) established the mandatory sequence:

    ```
    [1] Authorization — host country written authorization (prerequisite)
         ↓  ← triggers "first transfer" clock
    [2] First Transfer — first international transfer of ITMOs
         ↓  ← "transfer year" locked here
    [3] Corresponding Adjustment — host country deducts from NDC inventory
         ↓  ← must be completed before BTR review begins (COP29 clarification)
    [4] UNFCCC Reporting — reflected in Biennial Transparency Report (BTR)
    ```

    **COP29 clarification on First Transfer**: defined as "(a) first international transfer" or "(b) whichever of authorization / issuance / use / cancellation the host country designates — whichever is earliest." This means JCM credit transfer to Japan = first transfer.[^cop29]

    [^cop29]: Clyde & Co, "Article 6 developments at COP29" (Dec 2024): <https://www.clydeco.com/en/insights/2024/12/carbon-trading-and-article-6-at-cop29>; C2ES: <https://www.c2es.org/2025/02/why-the-cop29-article-6-decision-strengthens-high-integrity-carbon-markets/>

    ## Typical JCM Project Timeline

    | Phase | Time Required | Cumulative |
    |-------|--------------|------------|
    | Project design & approval | 12–24 months | ~2 yr |
    | Implementation & monitoring start | Immediate | — |
    | 1st monitoring period | 12–24 months | ~4 yr |
    | Third-party verification (TPE) | 3–6 months | ~5 yr |
    | JC registration & credit issuance | 3–6 months | ~5.5 yr |
    | Authorization (A6.2 type) | Parallel or +3–12 months | ~6 yr |
    | CA recorded in host BTR | Next BTR cycle | ~7–8 yr |

    **Implication**: A private JCM project registered in 2024–2025 will likely receive its first credits and CA recording no earlier than **2030–2032**.

    ## Six Structural Risks

    ### Risk ① — Issuance Lag

    JCM credits are issued based on "reductions achieved by December 31, 2030" — but actual credit issuance (verification complete → JC registration) will extend into 2031+. The treatment of credits *issued after 2030 but for pre-2030 reductions* is not yet fully clarified.[^moej]

    **Mitigation — Vintage Year Approach**: Article 6.2 guidance includes an option to record CA using the *vintage year* (year reductions occurred, ≤2030). This allows post-2030-issued credits to count for the 2030 NDC if the reduction was pre-2030.

    > "If ITMOs are based on the vintage year, this will allow all mitigation outcomes during the NDC implementation period to be included, even if they are first transferred after the NDC implementation period." — IGES[^iges]

    **Residual risk**: Whether the vintage year approach is adopted by each JCM Joint Committee (JC) depends on bilateral negotiation. If not adopted, last-minute 2030 reductions may not qualify for the 2030 NDC.

    [^moej]: JCM Implementation Guidelines (MOEJ/METI): <https://www.meti.go.jp/policy/energy_environment/global_warming/jcm/pdf/jp_Guidelines_for_the_Implementation_of_JCM.pdf>
    [^iges]: IGES, "Understanding Article 6.2 Reporting with the JCM": <https://www.iges.or.jp/en/pub/understanding-article62-reporting-jcm/en>

    ### Risk ② — BTR Timing (Reporting Cycle Risk)

    | Japan BTR | Submission Deadline | NDC Accounting Coverage |
    |-----------|--------------------|-----------------------|
    | BTR 1 | Dec 2024 | Through ~2022 |
    | BTR 2 | Dec 2026 | Through ~2024 |
    | BTR 3 | Dec 2028 | Through ~2026 |
    | BTR 4 | **Dec 2030** | **Through 2030 (NDC period end)** |

    Per COP29, CAs must be completed **before** BTR 4 review begins. Completing verification → issuance → transfer → CA recording for December 2030 reductions in time for the BTR 4 pre-review window is operationally near-impossible.

    **Safe zone**: Credits with reductions through 2026 (reflected in BTR 3) are operationally safe. Credits from 2029–2030 reductions face the highest timing risk.

    > "Corresponding adjustments now must occur prior to the initiation of the review of the first biennial transparency report containing the NDC period's end year." — Clyde & Co[^cop29]

    ### Risk ③ — CA Default Risk (Host Country)

    CA is a political act — the host country government must deduct authorized reductions from its own NDC inventory. Even after issuing Authorization, there is no automatic enforcement mechanism if a host country fails to perform the CA. CA completion can only be verified through the BTR review cycle (2+ years lag).[^wb]

    > "If a host country fails to provide corresponding adjustment evidence, the buyer may not be able to use the ITMO at all, leading to a loss equivalent to the money spent to purchase the ITMO." — World Bank[^wb]

    **Current mitigation options**:
    - Political Risk Insurance (PRI): proposed by World Bank; limited uptake in JCM projects
    - Explicit CA obligation clauses in bilateral IAs: standard in Singapore model; not yet universal in JCM agreements
    - JCMA (est. April 2025): centralises CA management, but cannot compel sovereign governments

    [^wb]: World Bank, "Managing the Risk of Corresponding Adjustment" (Jul 2021): <https://www.worldbank.org/en/news/feature/2021/07/14/managing-the-risk-of-corresponding-adjustment>

    ### Risk ④ — NDC Gap (2030–2035)

    NBS and long-term RE/EE projects generate reductions **beyond 2030**. But:

    - The current JCM framework targets the 2030 NDC
    - There is no authorization basis for ITMOs in 2031+ until host countries set their next NDC (2035 target)
    - Until next-NDC bilateral extensions are agreed, the 2030–2035 period is a regulatory blank

    > "There is a risk that airline operators may not be able to secure corresponding adjustments for CORSIA EEUs during the years 2030 to 2035 of the Second Phase unless the Host Country has a new NDC for the corresponding period." — HFW[^hfw]

    **NBS impact**: REDD+ and forest conservation projects have 30–50 year design lives. If CAs cannot be secured for the 2030–2035 period, that revenue window collapses to voluntary market (VCM) prices — the major investment deterrent for long-duration NBS projects.

    **Partial relief**: Japan's 2025 FY2035 NDC submission creates the legal basis for JCMA to negotiate JCM framework extensions with host countries — but actual bilateral extensions and new Authorizations will take 1–3 years.

    [^hfw]: HFW, "CORSIA & Article 6 — A Crucial Intersection in International Climate Policy": <https://www.hfw.com/insights/corsia-and-article-6-a-crucial-intersection-in-international-climate-policy/>

    ### Risk ⑤ — Banking Prohibition

    ITMOs **cannot be banked across NDC implementation periods**.

    - JCM-A6.2 credits issued in 2029–2030 that cannot be transferred/CA-applied before the 2030 deadline cannot be carried forward to the next NDC period (without vintage year approach)
    - This creates a risk of overshooting demand near 2030, followed by sudden drying-up of supply — a market cliff

    ### Risk ⑥ — Private JCM ROI Risk

    Under the private JCM model (introduced March 2023 — companies invest without government subsidy and receive credits):

    | Phase | Timing |
    |-------|--------|
    | Initial investment | 2024–2025 |
    | First credit issuance | 2026–2028 |
    | A6.2 Authorization obtained | 2027–2029 (optimistic) |
    | GX-ETS utilisation | 2028–2030 |
    | Full investment recovery | 2030+ (typically 5–8 yr projects) |

    If post-2030 credit revenue is uncertain (unclear GX-ETS / next-NDC framework), developers discount the post-2030 value heavily — making long-duration projects (especially NBS) economically borderline.

    > "There is a need to promote the formation of JCM projects invested and implemented by private companies without any governmental financial supports for the purpose of obtaining JCM credits." — MOEJ/GEC (2024)[^gec]

    [^gec]: JCM Recent Developments (MOEJ, May 2024): <https://www.env.go.jp/content/000129307.pdf>

    ## Practitioner Checklist

    **Pre-development**
    - [ ] Confirm host country's next NDC (2035 target) submission timeline — determines Authorization extension feasibility
    - [ ] Confirm JCM JC has adopted or will adopt vintage year approach
    - [ ] Design monitoring period so 1st monitoring completion is ≤2028

    **During development**
    - [ ] Authorization scheduled *before* first transfer
    - [ ] CA obligation explicitly stated in IA / JCM bilateral agreement
    - [ ] TPE (Third-Party Verifier) pre-booked accounting for verification lag

    **Financial planning**
    - [ ] Can investment be recovered from pre-2030 credits alone? (conservative base case)
    - [ ] Post-2031 revenues modelled as VCM/next-NDC — discounted appropriately
    - [ ] Political Risk Insurance considered for CA default

=== "🇯🇵 日本語"

    ## 概要

    JCM 2030年問題とは、一つの問題ではなく**6つの構造的リスクが複合した問題群**である。核心は「クレジット発行・CA（Corresponding Adjustment）取得・NDC会計の三つのタイムラインが連動せず、民間事業者が最大のリスクを負う構造」にある。

    | 指標 | 数値 |
    |------|------|
    | JCM 2030年目標（累積） | **100 Mt CO₂e** |
    | 2026年3月時点発行累積 | **817,247 t CO₂e**（≈ 0.82 Mt） |
    | 目標達成率 | **~0.82%** |
    | 年間必要ペース | **~22 Mt/年**（現状の約27倍） |
    | 日本2035年NDC | FY2035：FY2013比60%削減（2025年提出） |
    | JCMA設立 | 2025年4月 |

    ## Article 6.2 法的シーケンス

    ```
    [1] Authorization（ホスト国による書面承認） ← 必須前提
         ↓
    [2] First Transfer（ITMOの最初の国際移転）← transfer year確定
         ↓
    [3] Corresponding Adjustment（ホスト国がNDC目録から差引） ← BTRレビュー前に完了必須
         ↓
    [4] UNFCCC報告（BTR：二年次透明性報告）
    ```

    ## JCMプロジェクトの典型タイムライン

    | フェーズ | 所要時間 | 累積 |
    |---------|---------|------|
    | 設計・承認 | 12〜24ヶ月 | 〜2年 |
    | 第1モニタリング期間 | 12〜24ヶ月 | 〜4年 |
    | 第三者検証（TPE） | 3〜6ヶ月 | 〜5年 |
    | JC登録・クレジット発行 | 3〜6ヶ月 | 〜5.5年 |
    | A6.2 Authorization取得 | 並行または+3〜12ヶ月 | 〜6年 |
    | CAのBTR記録 | 次回BTRサイクル | 〜7〜8年 |

    **含意**: 2024〜2025年登録の民間JCMプロジェクトの最初の発行・CA記録は2030〜2032年以降になりうる。

    ## 6つの構造的リスク

    | リスク | 事業者への深刻度 | 緩和策の成熟度 |
    |--------|:----------:|:----------:|
    | ①クレジット発行ラグ | ★★★★ | 🟡 Vintage year option（未確定） |
    | ②BTRタイミング | ★★★ | 🟡 BTR 3への早期CA組込が実質的解決策 |
    | ③CA不履行リスク | ★★★★★ | 🔴 PRI普及途上；JCMA新設で一部改善 |
    | ④NDC間空白（2030〜35） | ★★★★ | 🟡 2035年NDC設定で改善見込み（交渉中） |
    | ⑤バンキング禁止 | ★★★ | 🔴 ルール上変更困難；vintage yearのみ回避可 |
    | ⑥民間JCM ROI | ★★★★★ | 🔴 2035年NDCフレームワーク延長で部分的に緩和 |

    ### ①クレジット発行ラグ
    2030年実現分の削減に基づくクレジットが2031年以降に発行された場合の扱いが未確定。**Vintage year approach**（削減実現年でCAを記録する方式）が採用されれば回避可能だが、各JC合同委員会での採否は交渉次第。

    ### ②BTRタイミング
    COP29の明確化によれば、CAはBTR 4（2030年12月）のレビュー開始「前」に完了しなければならない。2030年末の削減分を同年末までに発行・移転・CA記録することは現実的に不可能。**安全圏は2026年以前の削減分（BTR 3反映）**。

    ### ③CA不履行リスク
    CAはホスト国が自国NDC目録から削減量を差し引くという政治的行為。ホスト国がAuthorizationを発出後にCAを行わなかった場合の法的救済手段は乏しい（Worldbank, 2021）。政治リスク保険（PRI）・IA明文化・JCMAによる管理一元化が緩和策だが、普及は途上。

    ### ④NDC間空白（2030〜2035年）
    NBS・長期RE案件は2030年以降も削減を継続するが、次NDC（2035年目標）のAuthorizationが出るまで2031〜2035年の収益がVCMのみになるリスク。日本の2035年NDC設定で交渉基盤は生まれたが、実際の延長手続きに1〜3年かかる。**NBSプロジェクトには最も深刻なリスク**。

    ### ⑤ITMOバンキング禁止
    NDC実施期間をまたいだITMOのバンキングは原則不可。2029〜2030年に発行されたクレジットが2030年NDCに間に合わなかった場合、次期NDCへの持ち越しはできない（vintage year approachが適用されない場合）。

    ### ⑥民間JCM投資回収問題
    政府補助なしで先行投資する民間JCMモデルでは、2030年以降の収益可視性がなければ長期プロジェクトへの投資判断が困難。特にNBSプロジェクト（30〜50年設計寿命）では2030〜2035年の空白リスクが投資回収を直撃する。

    ## 日本政府の対応状況

    | 施策 | 状況 | 効果 |
    |------|------|------|
    | 2035年NDC（60%削減）提出 | ✅ 2025年 | JCMフレームワーク延長交渉の法的根拠 |
    | JCMA設立 | ✅ 2025年4月 | CA管理一元化（強制権限なし） |
    | PINプロセス（14カ国） | ✅ | 事前承認で発行ラグ短縮 |
    | 新規申請基準（2025年12月） | ✅ | A6.2シーケンシング要件組込済み |
    | Vintage year方式の標準化 | 🔴 未実現 | JC決定が必要；未解決 |
