    Sweden 🇸🇪 | スウェーデン — Article 6.2 Acquiring Country¶

    Overview¶

    Sweden is an active Article 6.2 acquiring country with bilateral agreements signed across Africa and Asia. Through the Swedish Energy Agency (Energimyndigheten), Sweden has established bilateral partnerships under the Paris Agreement with Dominican Republic, Ghana, Zambia, Rwanda, Kenya, and Nepal.1 Sweden's world-highest domestic carbon tax (SEK 1,330/t ≈ US$125) creates strong demand for cost-efficient international reductions.

    Item Detail
    NDC Target 63% reduction vs. 1990 by 2030 (Sweden's enhanced commitment beyond EU)
    Long-term target Net zero 2045; some sectors to -10%
    Carbon Tax SEK 1,330/t CO₂e (≈ US$125) — world's highest
    EU ETS Participates; ~€50–70/t for covered sectors
    Procurement mechanism Bilateral government agreements (Energimyndigheten)
    Procurement budget Not publicly disclosed; part of climate finance envelope
    Key Agency Swedish Energy Agency (Energimyndigheten)

    Carbon Pricing System¶

    Sweden operates two parallel carbon pricing systems: the world's highest domestic carbon tax (SEK 1,330/t ≈ US$125, covering non-ETS sectors including transport and heating) and EU ETS participation (covering large industrial emitters, electricity). Sweden's domestic carbon tax has been in place since 1991 and is a global policy model.

    Despite the high domestic price, Sweden uses A6.2 bilateral agreements to generate additional emission reductions internationally where costs are lower, effectively extending its climate ambition cost-efficiently. Sweden's long-term net-zero goal (2045) and negative-emission targets for some sectors create demand for internationally recognized credits.

    サマリー¶

    スウェーデンはスウェーデンエネルギー庁（Energimyndigheten）を通じて、アフリカ・アジアで6カ国との二国間A6.2協定を締結済みの調達国。1 世界最高水準の炭素税（SEK 1,330/t≈US$125）を国内で運用しつつ、コスト効率的な国際削減を追求する。

    NDC目標 2030年に1990年比63%削減（EUを上回る独自目標）

    炭素価格制度の概要¶

    世界最高水準の炭素税（SEK 1,330/t）は非ETS部門（交通・暖房）に適用。EU ETSは大規模産業排出者をカバー。スウェーデンは1991年から炭素税を導入したパイオニアで、高価格国内制度と低コストの国際調達を組み合わせた戦略を取る。
