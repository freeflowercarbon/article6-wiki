    IMO — International Shipping Carbon Market¶

    Overview¶

    The International Maritime Organization (IMO) governs GHG emissions from international shipping (~3% of global CO₂). The 2023 IMO GHG Strategy targets net zero by 2050. Current regulations (CII, EEXI) do not use carbon credits, but market-based measures (MBMs) with potential carbon credit components are under active development, targeting adoption by 2025–2027 and implementation by 2028.

    Unlike CORSIA, IMO has not yet adopted any carbon credit scheme. The framework is still being negotiated at the Marine Environment Protection Committee (MEPC).

    Item Detail

    GHG Strategy 2023 Strategy: net zero by 2050; 20% by 2030 vs 2008

    Current Regulations (No Carbon Credits)¶

    Regulation Since Type Credits?
    EEXI (Energy Efficiency Existing Ship Index) 2023 Technical standard No
    CII (Carbon Intensity Indicator) 2023 Operational rating (A–E) No
    IMO 2020 Sulphur Cap 2020 Fuel standard No

    Proposed Market-Based Measures¶

    Three main approaches under MEPC debate:

    Option A: GHG Fuel Standard + Feebate¶

    • Ships must meet lifecycle GHG intensity standard per fuel type
    • Under-compliance → pay penalty; over-compliance → receive credit/rebate
    • Carbon credits (A6.4 PACM?) potentially offsetting compliance shortfall

    Option B: Fixed Levy per Tonne CO₂¶

    • US$18–400/t range in proposals (IMO member states disagree widely)
    • Revenue pool → developing countries + clean shipping fund
    • Carbon offsets NOT likely to play a major role in this model

    Option C: ETS for Shipping¶

    • Cap-and-trade similar to EU ETS (which already covers some shipping)
    • Shipping allowances tradeable; offsets may be allowed to limited %

    Price Scenarios¶

    Scenario 2028 2030 2035
    Low (modest levy) US$18–30/t US$30–50/t US$50–80/t
    Mid (fuel standard) US$40–80/t US$60–120/t US$80–150/t
    High (aggressive levy) US$100+/t US$150+/t US$200+/t

    Prices are theoretical pending adoption.

    NDC Achievement / Sectoral Target¶

    Metric Status
    2030 intensity target -20% vs. 2008 (likely achievable with current CII measures)
    2050 net zero Large gap — requires MBMs + clean fuels + offsets
    GST implication International shipping under pressure; IMO expected to tighten 2023 strategy

    IMO and Article 6.2 Interface¶

    The potential role of carbon credits in IMO's MBM is an active frontier: - UNFCCC Article 6 credits (particularly A6.4ERs) could potentially serve as compliance instruments - Some proposals explicitly mention A6.4 PACM as a preferred credit source - Developing countries (AOSIS, SIDS) argue that shipping levy revenue should flow to climate finance — not just offset shipping emissions - Key tension: Using A6.2/A6.4 credits for shipping compliance could drain the same credit supply needed for national NDC achievement

    概要¶

    IMO（国際海事機関）は国際海運からのGHG排出（世界のCO₂の約3%）を管轄。2023年IMO GHG戦略は2050年ネットゼロを目標とする。現行規制（CII・EEXI）は炭素クレジットを使用しないが、炭素クレジット活用が見込まれる市場型メカニズム（MBM）が2025〜2027年の採択・2028年以降の実施を目指して交渉中。

    主要パラメータ¶

    項目 内容
    管轄機関 IMO（国際海事機関）
    GHG戦略 2023年戦略：2050年ネットゼロ、2030年に2008年比20%削減
    現行規制 CII（2023〜）、EEXI — 技術・運用基準（クレジット不使用）
    MBM提案の価格 US$18〜150+/t（提案により大幅な幅）
    MBM採択見込み 2025〜2027年（MEPC 83/84/85）
    MBM実施開始見込み 2028年〜
    A6.2/A6.4との関係 非公式協議中（正式決定なし）

    MBMとカーボンクレジットの関係¶

    IMOの市場型メカニズム交渉における炭素クレジットの位置づけ： - A6.4 PACM（PACMユニット）がMBMの適格オフセットとして認定される可能性が検討中 - 開発途上国は「levy収入を途上国気候基金に充当すべき」とし、クレジット方式に異議 - A6.2 ITMOを海運コンプライアンスに充当すると、国別NDC達成に使えるクレジット量が減少するトレードオフが存在
